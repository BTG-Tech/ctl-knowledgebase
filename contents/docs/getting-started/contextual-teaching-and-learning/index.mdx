## What is Contextual Teaching and Learning?

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/i3Xiy-1VcH4?si=EYSe-YLbD5ekIFpc" title="YouTube video player" frameBorder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" referrerPolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" allowFullscreen></iframe>

A pedagogy that is student-centered and focuses on providing students a meaningful and relevant way to connect what they already know and understand to 
what they are learning and relate that to the real world. Several implemetations of Contextual Teaching and Learning include:
- Goal Based Learning
- Project Based Learning
- Inquiry-oriented Learning 

## Constructivism and Active Learning

<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/YkPjTJ6L2RI?si=px1mBz-sah6KL-rS" title="YouTube video player" frameBorder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" referrerPolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" allowFullscreen></iframe>

CTL follows the constructivism theory in which learners construct knowledge through experience and social interaction, and incorporate those 
understandings with what they already know rather than passively and reactively acquiring knowledge.
